ROME

all roads lead to rome

in a nutshell

The Eternal City’s nickname is well-earned: it’s hard to walk down any Roman street and not see something ancient and magical. For lovers of art and architecture, it’s a wonderland. The Coliseum, the Forum, the Sistine Chapel, St. Peter’s Square… And if you get overloaded with the big attractions, head down almost any street and you’ll find new Renaissance palazzos and Baroque churches to explore. At the end of the day, stop at a bar for aperitivo, the local cure for a busy day, and you’ll understand what ‘la dolce vita’ is all about.

GETTING AROUND

Discover medieval alleys and small piazzas by walking or renting a bicycle. The subway is working nicely as well.

KNOWN FOR

The Colosseum, Pizza, Vatican City, Hidden Treasures, Gladiators, Vespas.

WHAT TO EAT

Fresh Pasta, Pizza (avoid the tourist traps), Gelato (avoid the tourist traps).

HOW MUCH

Dinner for 2 – 55€
Beer – 3.50€-6€

WHEN TO GO

Spring & Fall.
