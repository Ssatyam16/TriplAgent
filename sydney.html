SYDNEY

about as far as you can get

in a nutshell

Many people’s idea of Australia begins and ends on the beach. Arguably, that’s quite a compliment: we rightly think of Australians as relaxed, friendly people living in a place blessed with both good weather and natural beauty. But Oz has depth, too – and nowhere more so than Sydney. There’s a vibrant art scene, which includes contemporary work from across Asia. Some of the world’s best restaurants crowd the harbour – but watch out, the resident pelicans will be eager to share your supper. Then, of course, there’s the scalloped majesty of the Sydney Opera House: a rousing riposte to the notion that young countries lack culture.

GETTING AROUND

Fairly small city that you can explore on foot. The light rail/tram will take you to many different neighbourhoods.

KNOWN FOR

Sydney Opera, Bondi Beach, Sharks, Surfers (that are not afraid of sharks).

WHAT TO EAT

Not exactly known for it´s culinary scene. However, there are some top class spots if you know where to look.

HOW MUCH

Dinner for 2 – $40
Beer – $3-$5

WHEN TO GO

All year round, but avoid December to February when it´s really hot.
